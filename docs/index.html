Prema Sanskrit App

A small public entry point for learning Sanskrit-based ethical words through English, Japanese, and reflective dialogue.

Boiled eggs never hatch.

We are not here to re-cook the result. We are here to plant the Living Seed.

About This Project

This project is a linguistic entry gate for Prema OS: Sanskrit-Based Ethical Language Architecture.

It does not aim to replace traditional Sanskrit scholarship or religious practice. It is an applied experiment that reorganizes a limited set of Sanskrit-derived ethical words as a vocabulary for education, reflection, and AI ethics.

The First Public App

8-Word Cube App

The first implementation is a small browser-based app in which the user selects four important words from a core vocabulary and then pairs them with four opposite or complementary words.

These eight words are then carried into NotebookLM together with a diary entry or reflective note.

The full interactive version will be placed in this repository as the implementation proceeds.

Basic Flow

  1. Learn key words related to truth, goodness, and beauty.
  2. Select four words that matter to you now.
  3. Choose opposite or complementary words to form four axes.
  4. Bring the resulting eight-word set into NotebookLM for deeper reflection.

Guides

At the current stage, the active symbolic guides are Ganesha and Hanuman.

Future expansion may include Prince Shōtoku, Murasaki Shikibu, and Kūkai as Japanese cultural guides in the broader Prema OS framework.
