What this project is

This project is a small public experiment in ethical language, reflection, and learning. It is part of Prema OS, a framework that reorganizes a limited set of Sanskrit-based words as a practical vocabulary for education, self-understanding, and AI dialogue.

Why Japanese people use Sanskrit

Sanskrit is not foreign to Japan. Through Kūkai, Shingon, Siddhaṃ script, and Buddhist vocabulary, Sanskrit has been quietly rooted in Japanese culture for more than a thousand years. This project builds on this unique Japanese reception.
